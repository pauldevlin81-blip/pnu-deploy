If you've been refused — or you're about to fill out the form — this is for you.

Most PIP forms quietly lose for one reason. People answer the questions on the page honestly, but they answer the wrong question.

The form asks "can you wash and bathe yourself?" Most people answer yes. But the DWP isn't really asking that. They're asking whether you can wash and bathe to an acceptable standard, repeatedly, safely, and in a reasonable time period — on most days.

That's a completely different question. And the gap between what you can sometimes do and what you can do to that standard is where most PIP claims get scored at zero.

England and Wales only.

PIP in England and Wales is handled by the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP). If you're in Scotland, PIP has been replaced by Adult Disability Payment (ADP) — different system, different language, different forms. This guide is not for you. If you're in Northern Ireland, PIP is handled by the Department for Communities (DfC) — a separate guide exists for NI claimants.

Everything on this page is written specifically for the DWP process in England and Wales.

Questions people ask

Is this professional advice?

No. PIP Navigator provides peer support and educational guidance only. It's written by people who have been through the PIP system, not by solicitors or regulated advisers. For formal advice on your specific case, contact Citizens Advice or a regulated benefits adviser.

Will this guarantee I get awarded PIP?

No, and anyone telling you otherwise is lying. The PIP system is complex, evidence-based, and ultimately decided by a DWP assessor. This guide helps you describe your situation honestly and completely — in the language the system is set up to read. Whether you're awarded depends on your individual circumstances.

I'm in Scotland — can I use this?

No. Scotland replaced PIP with Adult Disability Payment (ADP) in 2022. The forms, descriptors, and process are different. This guide is written specifically for the DWP PIP process in England and Wales.

I'm in Northern Ireland — can I use this?

PIP in Northern Ireland is administered by the Department for Communities (DfC), not the DWP. The forms and process are similar but not identical. A separate guide exists for NI claimants — we'd rather you used the one written for your jurisdiction.
