Why this is for England & Wales only

This guide covers DWP PIP in England and Wales only.

Scotland now uses Adult Disability Payment (ADP) under Social Security Scotland — different forms, different rules, different descriptors. Northern Ireland uses PIP but administered by DfC, not DWP, with its own processes.

The wording patterns, assessment criteria, and mandatory reconsideration process in this guide are specific to the DWP system. If you live in Scotland or Northern Ireland, this is not written for your claim.

Common questions

Will this guarantee an award?

No. Nobody can guarantee a PIP decision — it depends on your condition, your evidence, and the assessor. What this does is help you describe your difficulties in the language the DWP system is designed to recognise, so the form actually reflects your life.

What if my condition isn’t listed?

The worked examples cover a range of conditions (chronic pain, depression, anxiety, MS, fibromyalgia, autism, ADHD, and more) — but the patterns are transferable. PIP is about what you can’t do, not your diagnosis. You adapt the wording pattern to your situation.

Is this for Scotland or Northern Ireland?

No. Scotland now uses ADP (Adult Disability Payment) with different rules. Northern Ireland uses PIP but under DfC, not DWP. This guide covers England and Wales only — the forms, descriptors, and challenge process are specific to DWP.

What if I’ve already been refused?

The mandatory reconsideration section is specifically for you. It covers how to identify where your form was scored incorrectly, what evidence to add, and how to structure the MR letter.
